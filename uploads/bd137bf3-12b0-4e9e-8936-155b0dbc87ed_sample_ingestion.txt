# BankMind AI Risk Management and Loan Policy Guidelines

## Section 1: Introduction and Objective
This document outlines the operational guidelines and regulatory policy compliance requirements for the BankMind AI automated credit scoring and document ingestion system. The primary goal of these guidelines is to establish a robust, reliable, and secure framework for processing applicant records, evaluating financial profiles, and ensuring complete alignment with global banking compliance standards. In the modern era of digitized financial technology, automated systems must handle substantial amounts of unstructured document data. By leveraging advanced parsing mechanisms like Apache Tika, we extract deep semantic features from files including PDFs, Microsoft Word documents, and text files. These extracted texts are subsequently processed, partitioned into chunks, and stored within relational databases (MySQL) and vector databases (Qdrant) to support real-time querying, information retrieval, and verification.

## Section 2: Scope and Applicability
These guidelines are strictly applicable to all personal, business, education, auto, and home loan processing pipelines within the BankMind AI ecosystem. All credit officers, software engineers, system architects, and compliance auditors are required to understand, implement, and maintain these policies. Under no circumstances should an automated decision override a regulatory rule unless a certified credit supervisor performs a manual override. The manual overrides are subject to strict multi-factor authentication and are permanently logged in the audit trail database. The integrity of applicant data must be preserved at every step of the ingestion pipeline.

## Section 3: Detailed Underwriting Criteria
The credit underwriting process inside BankMind AI utilizes a combination of traditional scoring models and deep learning engines. To ensure fairness and accuracy, the following baseline parameters are checked during the initial stage:
1. Minimum Age Requirement: The primary applicant must be at least 21 years of age and not older than 65 years of age at the time of loan application. For education loans, the student co-borrower can be 18 years of age, provided a parent or guardian serves as the primary financial guarantor.
2. Stable Income Proof: Salaried applicants must provide their last three months' salary slips and six months' bank statements. Self-employed applicants must show at least two years of audited financial statements, tax returns, and twelve months of active business account statements.
3. Credit Score Threshold: The minimum CIBIL score required for any unsecured personal loan is 720. For secured loans, such as home or auto loans, a score of 680 may be considered, subject to collateral valuation.
4. Fixed Obligation to Income Ratio (FOIR): The total monthly debt obligations of the applicant, including the proposed Equated Monthly Installment (EMI), must not exceed 45% of their net monthly take-home income. For high-income individuals (earning above $10,000 monthly), the FOIR threshold may be extended to 50% upon approval from the Risk Assessment Committee.

## Section 4: Data Privacy and Ingestion Pipeline Security
To comply with the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and regional financial data protection laws, the BankMind AI ingestion pipeline employs end-to-end encryption. All documents uploaded by customers are first stored in an encrypted temporary directory. The extraction service extracts the text content in-memory, and immediately afterwards, the original files are moved to a secured document repository with restricted access controls. The extracted text is processed using a semantic character-based or token-based text chunker. Chunks are designed to have overlapping windows (e.g., 500 characters with a 50-character overlap) to prevent the loss of contextual information across chunk boundaries. Each chunk is saved in the MySQL database under `document_chunks` and associated with a unique document record under `documents`. The system records the start time, completion time, status, and any error message of each sync operation inside `source_sync_logs` to maintain transparency and facilitate system debugging.

## Section 5: Regulatory Compliance and Anti-Bias Auditing
Algorithmic bias is a significant risk in automated financial decision-making systems. BankMind AI is committed to establishing a neutral, objective, and unbiased model. The training datasets and live inference logs are audited quarterly by an independent external regulatory compliance firm. The system does not utilize protected characteristics such as race, color, religion, national origin, sex, marital status, or age as inputs to the machine learning model. Furthermore, any anomaly in the document ingestion pipeline, such as empty extractions or abnormally large chunk sizes, triggers an automatic system alert to the IT operations desk. This document serves as the official baseline for the Phase 2.2 end-to-end ingestion pipeline verification testing.
